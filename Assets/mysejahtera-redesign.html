MYSEJAHTERA REDESIGN

MySejahtera is an app developed by the Malaysian government to assist in monitoring the COVID-19 situation in the country.

As the COVID-19 pandemic continues, I decide to re-examine the essential app that is required to be used by all Malaysians regularly.

*The Challenge

In February 2021, the government made it mandatory to use the MySejahtera app in areas with wide internet coverage. With cases still in the thousands, there doesn't seem to be any end in sight for the pandemic. The MySejahtera app has become an essential part of our daily lives. My aim is to re-examine and improve the overall experience of the app.

This redesign is in no way trying to undermine the hardwork and thought that has been put into this app, it is merely my own vision on how the app can be better.

*User Feedback

To get a better understanding of the user's reception towards the app. I decided to scour the internet for some comments. I used twitter and the apple app store comments section to gather my information.

Here are a few key insights:

UI Issues - Unstandardised language, small button size, lack of search bar.

Troublesome Check-In Experience - App takes awhile to load, checking-in with a dependent not intuitive.

Lack Of Details On Cases - Users want more specific details on cases especially cases which are in close proximity.

*Sketches & Wireframe

After gathering all information, I start to sketch out ideas and proceeded to create high fidelity wireframes in Figma.

Group Check-In - The current check-in experience consists of each individual user scanning the QR code to check-in. This can be a hassle when there are a lot of users checking in at the same time especially when the app takes a while to load, even more so on older phones. I am aware that there is an add dependent feature but it is buried too deep within the settings.

I decided to integrate a group check-in feature at the check-in page. Users can add and check-in with their contacts. The checked-in contacts will receive a notification on their app.

Check-In Ticket - The check-in ticket layout has also been redesigned to make it easier for premise attendants to check important information like location, time and date clearly and quickly without holding up a line. Other information like 'last sanitization' and 'cases within the last 14 days' are also added.

Detailed Nearby Cases - Shows detailed confirmed cases nearby users. Users will be able to key in a location and have red dots indicating confirmed cases within the area.

Notifications Tab - All tagged check-ins and related info on visited premise will be updated here. The 'Things to do' tab in the current home page will also be moved to the notifications tab.

Check-In Button - A more prominent check-in button is added at the bottom navigation bar with the updated MySejahtera 'heart' logo.

*Final Design

The final design of the app uses the Poppins typeface. The dark and light blue hues of the app was derived from the current MySejahtera logo. The logo was updated to be more simple and legible as an app icon.
