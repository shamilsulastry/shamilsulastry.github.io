WASH WHEREVER

Wash Wherever is an app where users can place a booking for a car wash from wherever they are. A car washing team will be dispatched to their desired location for a custom car wash based on their chosen preference.

This self-directed project aims to connect users and a car washing service in a simple, clear and easy-to-use mobile app.

*The Challenge

Living in a city that requires a lot of driving, some people do not have time to squeeze in a car wash within their busy schedules. Especially now since most of them are working from home. Wash Wherever aims to tackle this problem by letting users get a car wash at the click of a button from wherever they are.

In order for the app to be successful, I identified several key features in which the app needs to fulfill:

1. Be as simple as possible. The app will need to connect the user to a car washing team as fast as possible in the most simple and clear way.

2. Have a consistent and clear UI. The app needs to have a unique look whilst adhering to current mobile app conventions.

3. Customizable car wash experience. The app needs to ensure that users will be able to customize their wash settings according to their specific needs.

*The Process

I started the design process by sketching user flows, specifically for onboarding and booking a car wash. The process was just for me to throw rough ideas for me to refine and develop.

This process also helped me determine the most efficient way the app could work and troubleshoot some design errors that might occur.

During this process I also started to think about the layout of the pages (especially the dashboard) to prepare for the wireframing process later.

*User Flows & Sitemap

I proceeded to create the user flows in Figma which will inform the overall structure of the app.

The sitemap is color coded according to pages which are accessible by registered users or public users, as well as the fields which they need to fill.

*Wireframe & Final Design

After mapping out the structure of the app, I created a hi-fidelity wireframe and prototype in Figma to test it out on an actual phone screen.

The UI is designed based on the 8pt grid system to maintain visual consistency. To capture the modern and clean feel I decided to go for cool bluish hues with a Montserrat typeface.
